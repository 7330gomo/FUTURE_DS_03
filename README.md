# Customer Conversion Funnel Analysis Project

## Project Overview

This project focused on analyzing customer behaviour across the full marketing funnel, from visitors to leads and finally to converted customers. The main goal was to understand how users interact with the business across different referral channels and how customer behaviour changes over time.

The analysis explored visitor activity, lead generation, conversion performance, and customer drop-off rates across channels such as Google, Direct, Email, and Social Media. The project aimed to identify strong and weak areas within the customer journey to support better marketing and business decisions.

---

# Project Objectives

The main objectives of the project were to:

* Analyze customer behaviour across the marketing funnel
* Measure visitor, lead, and customer conversion performance
* Identify top-performing referral channels
* Compare monthly performance trends throughout the year
* Measure traffic-to-lead and lead-to-customer conversion rates
* Identify customer drop-off points in the funnel
* Provide insights to improve customer acquisition and conversions

# Key Insights

The analysis revealed several important business insights:

* Google was the strongest referral channel, consistently generating the highest visitors, leads, and converted customers.
* Direct and Email channels also performed well and stayed close to Google in overall performance.
* Social Media performed weaker compared to other referral channels.
* July and August recorded the highest visitor traffic, leads, and customer conversions.
* January, February, and March showed the weakest overall performance with low traffic and conversions.
* Customer behaviour and business performance changed significantly throughout the year.
* Some months experienced high visitor and lead drop-off rates, indicating weaknesses in the customer journey.
* Strong traffic does not always result in strong customer conversions, highlighting funnel inefficiencies.

---

# Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were made:

* Focus more on Google, Direct, and Email channels because they generate the highest visitors, leads, and customers.
* Improve Social Media marketing through better content, audience targeting, and customer engagement strategies.
* Study successful strategies used during July and August and apply them to weaker months like January and February.
* Improve lead follow-up processes to reduce customer drop-offs before conversion.
* Allocate marketing budgets more effectively by investing in both peak-performing and low-performing months.
* Monitor funnel performance regularly to identify and fix weak points in the customer journey.
* Use customer behaviour insights to improve conversion consistency throughout the year.

---

# Conclusion

The analysis shows that business performance changes significantly across different months and referral channels. Google is the strongest channel because it consistently drives the highest visitors, leads, and converted customers. Direct and Email also perform strongly, making them important contributors to stable traffic and conversions.

Social Media, however, underperforms compared to the other channels and requires improvement to contribute more effectively to business growth.

Monthly analysis revealed that July and August are the strongest-performing months, generating the highest traffic, leads, and conversions. In contrast, January, February, and March perform poorly with low traffic and minimal conversions.

The findings show that business performance is heavily influenced by seasonal trends and marketing effectiveness. The customer funnel also contains weaknesses where many visitors fail to become leads or customers, especially during high drop-off periods.

Overall, the business has strong growth potential due to its high-performing channels and successful peak months. By improving weak-performing months, strengthening Social Media performance, and reducing customer drop-offs, the business can achieve more stable growth and better long-term performance.
